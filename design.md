## The Business in One Sentence

Deploy private, domain-aware AI agents for small service businesses as a managed
monthly service — each agent knows the client's own pricing, policies, and workflows
— with a long-term path to an automated multi-tenant deployment platform.

---

## Problem Statement

Small service businesses cannot scale their domain knowledge to customer interactions.
Generic AI tools fail because they lack company-specific context. A junk removal
company's pricing by volume and item type, an auto repair shop's labor rates, a
plumber's service area zip codes — none of that exists in ChatGPT.

The result: missed leads, wasted staff time on repetitive Q&A, inconsistent answers,
and after-hours calls that go to voicemail and never get returned.

The platform solves this by turning each client's own documents and knowledge into a
private, hosted AI agent that handles customer Q&A, lead capture, staff support, and
intake workflows — without their data ever leaving the controlled environment.

## Target Industries

NOT CRE-only. All owner-operated small service businesses with high call volume:

| Industry | Core pain | Revenue signal |
|---|---|---|
| Junk removal | Pricing by volume/item, service area, what they take | Missed calls = lost jobs |
| Auto repair | Labor/parts pricing, scheduling, status updates | Customers call 3 shops; first to answer wins |

## Premises

1. AGREED — Pain is real. Businesses lose revenue from inability to scale domain
   knowledge to customer interactions.
2. AGREED — Generic AI does not solve it. Company-specific context is required.
3. AGREED — Monthly recurring model is viable. Ongoing hosting and maintenance
   is how value is delivered — not a one-time install.
4. UNCERTAIN — Build speed and economics. If onboarding takes weeks per client,
   margins collapse. Needs validation from first 2–3 manual deployments.
5. UNCERTAIN — Moat durability. Embedded knowledge should create high switching
   cost, but churn behavior has not been observed yet.

## Three Approaches

### Approach A: Managed Service First
Sign 3 clients manually. $3,000 setup + $500/month. Learn what is repeatable.
- Effort: Small (1–2 weeks to first client)
- Risk: Low
- Con: Doesn't scale without the founder

### Approach B: Automated Deployment Platform (Platform-First)
Build the agent factory — meta-agent that interviews a client, ingests their docs,
deploys their RAG stack automatically. Tooling is the product.
- Effort: Large (2–3 months to v1)
- Risk: High — must do A first to know what to automate

### Approach C: Warm Contacts → Manual Service → Platform (Recommended)
Start with junk removal owner and auto repair contact. Deploy manually. Learn the
repeatable pattern. Then productize.
- Effort: Medium (2–3 weeks to first pilot)
- Risk: Medium
- Pro: Warm contacts remove cold outreach as the first bottleneck

---

## Recommended Path

**Warm contacts → Manual (A) → Platform (B)**

The platform pathway in detail — three questions must be answered first:
1. What does the repeatable onboarding pattern actually look like?
2. What document quality do small businesses actually have?
3. Who is the platform sold to — the business directly, or agencies who serve them?

The agency angle (B2B2B) is the highest-scale version: build deployment
infrastructure that IT consultants and MSPs use to deploy agents for their own SMB
clients. You charge per managed agent. They take the margin. Revisit after first
3 manual clients.

Founders who skip manual work and go straight to building the factory build the
wrong factory. The manual clients are the research.

## Build Order (Technical)

Week 1–2: Generic RAG knowledge agent
  — Web chat interface
  — PDF/text/FAQ ingestion
  — Accurate retrieval and response
  — Works for any industry

Week 3: Wrap intake flow around it
  — Lead capture and qualification
  — Routing and scheduling handoff
  — Uses existing n8n intake automation as base

Week 4+: Multi-tenant isolation
  — Separate pgvector schema per client
  — Tenant-scoped auth and access control
  — Admin dashboard for owner

After first 3 clients: Automated onboarding platform
